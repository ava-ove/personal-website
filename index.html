Calculating and Graphing Spatial Variation in River Power Rates

AUGUST.2019-PRESENT


Current research in seismology is trying to identify active faults that have potential to cause destructive earthquakes. Different techniques are used for this purpose. One of these techniques is to calculate the spatial variation in river power rates. The rivers are sensitive to the forces that cause earthquakes. When a river cuts an active fault, the landscape can be dramatically changed by water flow. I was partly involved in the NAME research to calculate and graph spatial variation in the rate of the stream power for the Kol River in the Zagros, Iran.

One of the reasons that made me interested in this project is the stream power concept and the relationship between topographic changes and the width of a channel. This might be useful to constrain a landscape model on some parts of Mars with young runoff channels using SRTMs.
